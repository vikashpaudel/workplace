About Us

The Department of Dermatology and Venereology at Patan Academy of Health Sciences (PAHS) is dedicated to improving skin health. Our department provides standardized treatments for various skin conditions and conducts research to support education and continuous improvement in patient care.

Our Commitment

We are committed to patient-centered care, education, and research, making us a leader in advancing the field of dermatology in Nepal. Our goal is to make a difference in the lives of those we serve and improve the health and well-being of our communities.

Patient Care

The Department of Dermatology at PAHS is dedicated to providing high-quality care to all patients, regardless of their socio-economic status. We believe that every person deserves access to the best possible medical care, and we strive to make this a reality for those in need. Our faculty members and staff are committed to providing compassionate and professional care to all patients.

Services Provided

  • Treatment for infections, psoriasis, contact dermatitis, and other skin, hair, and nail diseases.
  • Clinical and epidemiological research.
  • Affordable dermatological care.
  • Scientific and evidence-based care for skin diseases, sexually transmitted infections, and HIV/AIDS.
  • All dermatosurgical procedures are appointment-based.
  • Removal of moles, freckles, warts, skin tags, and tattoos using LASERS.
  • Reduction/Removal of unnecessary hairs using LASERS.
  • LASER Therapies (Fractional and Ablative Co2, Q switched Nd YAG, Long Pulse Nd YAG).
  • Electrofulguration/Electrocauterization, Cryotherapy.
  • Scar surgeries.
  • Split ear repair.
  • Narrow Band Ultraviolet therapy for vitiligo, psoriasis, etc.
  • Chemical cauterization.

Academic Activities

Undergraduate (UG) Program

Junior clerkship, Internship.

Post Graduate (PG) Program

All trainees/residents are actively involved in academics through case presentations, seminars, and journal presentations. The academic roster is updated yearly and released one month prior to allow trainees ample time for preparation.

The PG residency program is a competency-based program trained and evaluated across PAHS's eight core competencies.

The assessment tools used include: Core content review, Case-based discussion, DOPS, Mini-CEX, EPAs, Fieldnotes.
